What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a field of computer science focused on creating systems and programs capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence: understanding language, learning, reasoning, pattern recognition, decision making, and more.

Modern AI tools include:

  • ChatGPT — a conversational AI by OpenAI, able to chat, answer questions, help with coding, creativity, and learning.
  • Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion — image generation from text descriptions.
  • Copilot, Tabnine — intelligent coding assistants.
  • Google Bard, Gemini, Claude — other modern language and multimodal models.

Current AI trends:

  • Rapid development of large language models (LLMs) like ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude.
  • Integration of AI into everyday services, business, and education.
  • Growth of generative models for text, images, music, and video.
  • Focus on ethics, transparency, and AI safety.
  • Development of tools for automation, data analysis, and creativity support.

Q: What is the concept behind learning with a teacher?
A: Learning with a teacher, also known as supervised learning, involves training a model on a dataset where each input is paired with the correct output (label). The model learns to predict the output from the input by minimizing the difference between its predictions and the true labels. For example, in handwriting recognition, the model is shown images of handwritten digits along with the correct digit labels, allowing it to learn how to recognize new digits in the future.

Q: What is pattern recognition?
A: Pattern recognition is the process by which a computer system identifies regularities, trends, or structures in data. It enables machines to classify input data (such as images, sounds, or text) into categories based on learned patterns. For example, pattern recognition is used in handwriting recognition, where the system learns to distinguish different letters and numbers from images of handwritten text.

Q: What is the "Turing test"?
A: The Turing test, proposed by Alan Turing in 1950, is a method for determining whether a machine can exhibit intelligent behavior indistinguishable from that of a human. In the test, a human judge interacts with both a machine and a human without knowing which is which. If the judge cannot reliably tell the machine from the human, the machine is said to have passed the test.

О: Тест Тьюринга, предложенный Аланом Тьюрингом в 1950 году, — это способ определить, может ли машина проявлять интеллектуальное поведение, неотличимое от человеческого. В тесте человек-эксперт общается с машиной и человеком, не зная, кто есть кто. Если эксперт не может отличить машину от человека, считается, что машина прошла тест.
Q: What is the main purpose of the "Turing test"?
A: The main purpose of the Turing test is to evaluate a machine's ability to demonstrate human-like intelligence in conversation. It serves as a benchmark for assessing whether artificial intelligence can convincingly imitate human responses and behavior in a given context.
В: Какова основная цель теста Тьюринга?

Q: Explain the difference between strong and weak artificial intelligence. Give examples.
A: Strong AI, also known as artificial general intelligence (AGI), refers to machines that possess the ability to understand, learn, and apply knowledge across a wide range of tasks at a human level or beyond. Weak AI, or narrow AI, is designed to perform specific tasks and lacks general reasoning abilities. For example, a chess-playing program is weak AI, while a hypothetical robot that can learn any intellectual task like a human would be strong AI.

For example, in email spam detection, the model is trained on emails labeled as "spam" or "not spam" and learns to classify new emails accordingly.

Q: What is the activation function in a neural network and what is its role?
A: The activation function in a neural network introduces non-linearity into the model, allowing it to learn complex relationships in the data. Without activation functions, neural networks would only be able to model linear relationships. Common activation functions include ReLU, sigmoid, and tanh, each helping the network capture different types of patterns.

Q: Describe the main stages of the machine learning process, starting with data collection.
A: The main stages of the machine learning process are: (1) Data collection, where relevant data is gathered; (2) Data preprocessing, which involves cleaning and transforming the data; (3) Model training, where the algorithm learns from the data; (4) Model evaluation, where performance is assessed using metrics; and (5) Deployment, where the trained model is used in real-world applications.
В: Опишите основные этапы процесса машинного обучения, начиная со сбора данных.
О: Основные этапы машинного обучения: (1) сбор данных; (2) предобработка (очистка и преобразование); (3) обучение модели; (4) оценка качества с помощью метрик; (5) внедрение модели в реальное приложение.
Q: What is overfitting in machine learning and how can it be dealt with?
A: Overfitting occurs when a machine learning model learns the training data too well, including its noise and outliers, resulting in poor performance on new, unseen data. It can be addressed by using techniques such as cross-validation, regularization, pruning, or by collecting more training data. Simpler models are also less prone to overfitting.
В: Что такое переобучение в машинном обучении и как с ним бороться?
О: Переобучение — это ситуация, когда модель слишком хорошо запоминает обучающие данные, включая шум и выбросы, и плохо работает на новых данных. С этим борются с помощью кросс-валидации, регуляризации, упрощения модели, увеличения объёма данных или обрезки (pruning).
Q: Explain the concept of "deep learning" and how it differs from classical machine learning.
A: Deep learning is a subset of machine learning that uses artificial neural networks with many layers (hence "deep") to automatically learn representations and features from large amounts of data. Unlike classical machine learning, which often requires manual feature engineering, deep learning models can extract complex patterns directly from raw data, such as images or text. Deep learning has enabled breakthroughs in fields like speech recognition, image classification, and natural language processing.

Глубокое обучение позволило добиться прорыва в распознавании речи, изображений и обработке языка.

A: Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a field of AI that focuses on enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language. NLP tasks include machine translation (e.g., Google Translate), sentiment analysis (detecting positive or negative opinions in text), text summarization, and chatbots that can converse with users.

Q: Explain how a simple classification algorithm works, for example, the nearest neighbors (k-NN) method.
A: The k-Nearest Neighbors (k-NN) algorithm classifies a new data point by finding the k closest points in the training data and assigning the most common class among them. For example, to classify a new email as spam or not spam, k-NN looks at the most similar emails in the dataset and uses their labels to make a decision. The algorithm is simple, intuitive, and works well for small datasets.
В: Объясните, как работает простой алгоритм классификации, например, метод ближайших соседей (k-NN).
О: Алгоритм k-ближайших соседей (k-NN) классифицирует новый объект, находя k ближайших точек в обучающей выборке и присваивая наиболее частый класс среди них. Например, чтобы определить, является ли письмо спамом, k-NN ищет похожие письма и использует их метки для решения. Алгоритм прост, интуитивен и хорошо работает на небольших данных.
Q: What are the main ethical issues related to the development and application of artificial intelligence?
A: Key ethical issues in AI include bias and discrimination (when models reflect or amplify societal biases), privacy concerns (due to data collection and surveillance), job displacement (as automation replaces human labor), and accountability (who is responsible for AI decisions).
